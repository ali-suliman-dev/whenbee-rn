Category-detail hero, in situ (tier pill + number + maturity meter). 0 & B keep their treatment; R1–R4 ditch the band entirely, grounded in the research below. Same data throughout: range 5–20, lands ~13, guess was 8, 2 of 6 runs logged.

What the research says

Lead with one number. Glanceable UX = instant understanding, minimal cognitive effort. A chart you must decode fails the glance; the single most-likely value wins.

Show uncertainty in words. Nielsen: the rougher the estimate, the more you must flag its uncertainty — but as a clear outcome, not jargon or decoration. Range = a plain-language cue.

ETA-window pattern. Maps & delivery apps show a text range + one "typical/most-likely" anchor. They almost never draw an abstract distribution bar.

Sources: UX Tigers / Jakob Nielsen “Think-Time UX”; uxdesign.cc “Glanceable UX”; Prototypr “Expressing Time in UI/UX”.
