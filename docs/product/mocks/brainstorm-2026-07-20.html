01

Where the calendar refresh lives

Today the app fetches calendar events once per day-selection and never again (useDayCapacity.ts:122). Add an event in iOS Calendar while Whenbee is open and it will not appear. There is no refresh anywhere, and no pull-to-refresh in the whole app.

What successful apps actually do: Fantastical, Apple Calendar and Google Calendar have no visible refresh button at all — they refetch on app foreground and on screen focus, plus pull-to-refresh as the escape hatch. Sunsama and Notion Calendar do show one explicit sync control, always paired with a last-synced timestamp — because a refresh button with no staleness signal just tells the user "this data might be wrong" without telling them when.

So the real answer is two-part: (1) auto-refetch on foreground + focus — this is the missing 90%, and it is not a design question; (2) exactly one manual control, in the densest calendar surface. Three refresh buttons across Today / Honest Day / Plan would read as an app that doesn't trust its own data.

03

Done-by shouldn't dictate start-by

First — the diagnosis, because your premise was slightly off.
There is no 30-minute lock. The planner's floor was literally now. What you're actually hitting is that backwardFill packs tasks as late as possible, filling right-to-left from your done-by. Done-by 15:30 → it fills 14:00–15:30 first, then spills backwards past the 13:00 meeting → start-by 11:00.

And dragging a task only rewrites its sequence order (dayTasksStore.ts:441) — never which free window it sits in. The engine then re-derives placement from scratch, so your drag "before the meeting" gets undone the next frame. That is the lock you're feeling. There is no per-task window pinning in the model at all.

Already shipped this session: MIN_START_LEAD_MIN = 5 — the plan will now never claim you must have started earlier than 5 minutes from right now.
