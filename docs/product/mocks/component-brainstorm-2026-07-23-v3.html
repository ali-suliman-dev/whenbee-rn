Why the dark yellow feels off, and the trade-offs:
  • amberText #8A5A12 exists because real honey amber (#EEAE4D) fails contrast on white text-sized — darkened to pass AA it turns muddy brown. Any "amber text on white" ends up in that swamp.
  • C1 escapes the trap: text never carries the color; honey shows up as actual honey (bar, hex). Calmest, most premium.
  • C2 is clean + on-brand, but indigo elsewhere means "tappable" — a colored non-link risks dead taps.
  • C3 keeps warmth and reads intentional (same grammar as the "Ripens at" pill you already have) — dark yellow only ever on cream, never bare.
Recommendation — C1. The "hideous" wasn't amber, it was amber-as-text on white. Keep numbers ink like every other number in the app and let honey appear only as material (mini bar + hex). If you want more warmth than C1, C3's pill is the safe middle.
