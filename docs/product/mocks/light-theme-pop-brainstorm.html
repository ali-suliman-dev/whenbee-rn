Light theme — making it pop (research-driven brainstorm)

Research across Headspace, Gentler Streak, Amie, Finch, Duolingo, Structured, Waterllama, Opal: the "flat and boring" read comes from pure-white cards on a lavender ground with only two surface steps and neutral greys. The fix best-in-class apps use is not more color — it's a brand-tinted neutral ramp (every neutral leans indigo), one floating element with a soft brand-tinted shadow, a hero-area gradient wash where the eye lands first, and honey rendered as a material (gradient + highlight), not a flat fill. All four directions below keep the invariants: amber stays reserved for honey/reward, one filled indigo per screen, cards stay borderless.

Current (baseline)

Today, exactly as the tokens render now: #F4F2FC ground, pure-white cards, neutral greys, flat everywhere.

Why it reads flat:
  • Cards are pure #FFFFFF — clinical against the lavender, no hue unity.
  • Only 2 visible surface steps; nothing floats, nothing recedes.
  • Muted text (#5C5F73 / #9B9DAB) is hue-neutral grey — fights the indigo brand.
  • Amber ring is a flat conic fill — honey with no substance.

Token-only change. Deeper lavender ground, lavender-white cards (never pure white), indigo-tinted greys, and ONE soft indigo-tinted shadow — on the focal Next card only.
