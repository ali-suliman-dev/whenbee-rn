Copy rebuilt with conversion psychology (goal-gradient: lead with what's already IN, keep the next step countable and near) + humanizer (no em dash, no emoji in copy, short flat sentences) + retention framing (progress feels owned, never owed). Locked meanwhile: recap headline phrase = plain accent #EEAE4D text (your call, applied), REALLY TOOK rename, dark reward 1·I flow.

Copy + structure:
  • Sub: "Your logs switch these on, one by one." — mechanism + agency in eight words; no "when your logs can back it up" clause-stacking.
  • Ready status: "In" — shorter and warmer than "Ready", pairs with "2 logs away".
  • Micro-bar shows THIS read's own progress (real: logs toward its threshold) — goal-gradient on the exact object, not a card-level abstraction.
  • Footer: "No streak, no rush. It ripens as you log." — six + five words, flat and human.

Copy + structure:
  • Title becomes the progress claim: "One read in. Three ripening." — the endowment lands in the headline (you already OWN one), the strongest goal-gradient placement.
  • Sub: "Every log pours a little more in." — the honey metaphor doing the persuasion, no numbers repeated.

  • Risk: two progress systems on one screen (tally + honey ring above) — check on device.

Copy + structure:
  • Callout survives but in neutral clothes: sunken well, ink text, one small hex as the only warm pixel. The old version failed because it was amber-on-amber-on-amber.
  • "Next: What steals your time. Two honest logs and it opens." — names the prize, counts the distance, stops. "Opens" beats "unlocks" (no lock ever existed).
  • Slight redundancy: the same "2 logs" appears in the row above — the callout earns its place only if it adds the feature's name recognition; kill it if it feels doubled on device.
Recommendation — R1 inline momentum. It deletes the section you hate instead of repainting it, and moves the goal-gradient onto the exact row that's about to open — progress reads as a property of the thing, not a marketing banner. R2 is the boldest (headline owns the win) but doubles up with the honey ring above the card; R3 is the safest evolution if you still want a named "next" callout. Copy shared by all three: "Your logs switch these on, one by one." / status ladder In → 2 logs away → 5 logs → about a week / "No streak, no rush. It ripens as you log."
