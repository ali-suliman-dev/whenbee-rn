Brainstorm v6 — R2, copy made plain

What was unclear: "read" is our jargon (nobody calls a feature a "read"), "ripening" alone explains nothing, and "Every log pours a little more in" is poetry, not information. Three copy takes below on the SAME approved R2 card. Every line now answers the three questions a stranger has: what are these, what do I do, how far is the next one.

〰Patterns
Line by line:
  • Title: "Your first Pro feature is ready." — endowment ("your", "ready") + zero jargon. A stranger parses it in one pass.
  • Sub: "The other three open as you log more tasks." — counts the rest, names the mechanism. Nothing left to decode.
  • Statuses: "Ready" / "2 logs to go" / "5 logs to go" / "about a week" — one consistent countdown unit.
  • Footer: "No streak, no rush. Logging normally gets you there." — the reassurance, in the same plain register.

Recommendation — Copy A. It's the only one where every single line survives the stranger test with zero brand vocabulary required: what (Pro features), state (first one ready), mechanism (log more tasks), distance (2 logs to go), reassurance (no streak, no rush). B if you want the count to hit hardest (then de-dupe the tally chip); C if you want one ripening beat kept. Statuses and pips identical across all three — only words differ.
